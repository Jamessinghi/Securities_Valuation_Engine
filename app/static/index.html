    How the valuation engine works

    This tool turns company filings and market data into a broad, auditable valuation. It extracts the financial inputs, normalises them into AUD, runs 65 valuation methods, and triangulates the usable per-share estimates into a single reference value.

    The five-step workflow

    1. StockEnter an exchange and ticker, such as ASX:STO or NASDAQ:AAPL.
    2. DateChoose the valuation date; the engine determines the applicable period and trailing four completed quarters.
    3. DocumentsUpload annual, half-year, results, and quarterly PDFs. Text extraction and OCR identify the required financial line items.
    4. ManualReview anything the extractor could not read confidently and enter only the missing values.
    5. ValuationReview every method, its status, assumptions, notes, fair value per share, and the triangulated result.

    Document extraction

    Statement rows are ranked above prose and note references. Cash-flow context identifies operating cash flow and investing-activity CapEx, including CapEx split across several asset rows.

    Free cash flow

    DCF methods prefer operating cash flow minus cash-flow-statement CapEx. D&A is a labelled fallback when CapEx is unavailable, while company-reported FCF is retained as a cross-check.

    Currency consistency

    Reporting-currency amounts are converted to AUD at the valuation-date FX rate so intrinsic value can be compared directly with an AUD market price.

    Method coverage

    The engine runs 65 approaches across cash flow, dividends, earnings, assets, multiples, returns, scenarios, terminal value, and market-implied analysis.

    Transparent results

    Each method is marked computed, partial, or unavailable. Notes expose assumptions, proxies, missing external data, and any currency warning instead of silently filling gaps.

    Triangulation

    The headline intrinsic value is the median of the methods that produce a usable per-share estimate, reducing dependence on any single model.

    Important: This is an educational valuation aid, not personalised financial advice. Results depend on source-document quality, market-data availability, and model assumptions. Review the underlying filings and method notes before relying on any output. This product uses the FRED® API but is not endorsed or certified by the Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis.
